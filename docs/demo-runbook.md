# Faheem — Stage Script (Amad 2026 finals)

Print this. Keep it on the podium. The prompts are printed so you can follow along, NEVER so you can retype them: on stage, everything fires from the ⌘K palette or the `/` skill invoker. The only thing you ever type by hand is the Live Model trap in Scene 2 (it is a local calculation, not a chat request).

**The story you are telling: Ask → Verify → Produce → Decide.**
Four deals: Jahez (public, real), Darb (private data room), Thara Pay (at committee), Aqar Development (declined last year, the backup card).

1. App running in cached mode. Press **⌘.** and confirm the overlay says CACHED.

## Scene 1 — Jahez: every number has a source (~90s)

**SAY:** "This is Faheem. Four deals in our pipeline. Let's start with the public one, Jahez, and the one rule everything here obeys: every number is one click from its source."

**DO:** Jahez workspace → ⌘K → fire:

> Break down Jahez's FY2025 unit economics from #FY2025-Earnings-Release: GMV growth vs take rate, AOV, contribution margin, EBITDA margin, and why did net income compress ~61% despite double-digit GMV growth?

**WATCH:** the answer streams, citation chips on every figure.

**CLICK:** any citation chip. The PDF opens at the cited page, passage highlighted.

**SAY:** "The quoted text has to physically exist on that page or it can't be cited. A fake reference is structurally impossible."

**FOLLOW-UP (fire it, this is the killer):**

> Is the SAR 55M of Q4 one-offs genuinely non-recurring? Break it down.

**SAY:** "Notice, our own analyst pushes back on management's framing: only about 20 million is cleanly one-off, and the credit losses already repeat in Q1. The system audits; it doesn't parrot."

**SPARE follow-up:** *"How exposed is Jahez to Keeta's expansion through 2026?"*
**SPARE flourish (Arabic room):** flip locale to العربية → fire: *"هل يجتاز جاهز فحص الامتثال وفق ميثاق لجنة الاستثمار؟"* Full RTL, same citations.

## Scene 2 — `/dcf`: the model is a skill, and it's a real spreadsheet (~2 min)

**SAY:** "Analysts don't want a chat window, they want their playbooks. Ours are installed as skills, invoked like a command."

**DO:** in the composer type **/** → skills popover → pick **DCF, FCFF Build**. It fires:

> Run the DCF on Jahez out to 2031 and build the bull and bear cases against base.

**WATCH:** specialist teams choreograph → bull / base / bear strip against the 15% Benchmark → the Excel model appears in the sheet rail.

**DO:** open the workbook. Click a WACC cell.

**SAY:** "Look at the formula bar: a real formula, not a pasted number. And the cell comment: source, page. This is a working model your analyst edits on Monday, not an export of a chat."

**DO:** jump to the Live Model → tap a suggested assumption chip → WACC, DCF, IRR, and the sensitivity grid recompute in front of them.

**TRAP (optional, for a skeptical face):** type into the model bar: *Change FY25 revenue to SAR 2 billion* → Critical Review refuses.

**SAY:** "Sourced actuals are locked. Only analyst assumptions move. Two kinds of numbers, and the system knows which is which."

**FOLLOW-UPS (pick one by judge mood, all recorded):**
- > Walk me through the WACC build for the Jahez valuation: where does every input come from?
- > The trading comps imply a much higher value than your DCF. Why the gap, and which should the committee trust?
- > What would have to be true for Jahez to re-rate to your bull case?

## Scene 5 — IC room: compare and decide (~90s)

**SAY:** "Now the room where it lands. The committee, all four deals on the table."

**DO:** IC room → fire:

**SAY:** "The committee stress-tests the analysis against GASTAT data before the vote, not after."

> Rank these deals: strongest risk-adjusted case?

**WATCH:** the comparison table, all four names. The answer argues Thara Pay over Jahez out loud: a wider cushion above the Benchmark, on cleaner inputs, while Jahez leans on an unfinished WACC.

**POINT AT:** the persistent line: "Advisory only, the investment decision rests with the committee."

**SAY:** "Faheem argues. Humans decide. That's the design, not a disclaimer."

## If something breaks (three graceful lines)

1. **Network dies / API hangs:** "Let me switch us to our verified layer" (tap ⌘.) "...and this is exactly why regulated institutions need a deterministic, audited path. The entire demo runs air-gapped."
2. **A figure gets challenged:** "Good catch, and notice our own fact-checker already flags that. Every figure here is one click from its source." Then click the citation.
3. **Freeze or crash:** "One second, everything here runs locally and deterministically, so nothing is lost." Reload calmly. ⌘K reopens the exact question. **Never retype.**
