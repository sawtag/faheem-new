# Faheem — Judge Q&A Pack (Amad 2026 finals)

**For:** the founder (on stage), Alinma Bank × Tuwaiq Academy finals, Riyadh, July 16–18.
**Judges:** fintech professionals + bank executives. They will probe the AI, the finance, and the business model.
**Rule of the room:** you are a smart generalist, not a quant — and that's fine. Your edge is that **the product proves everything for you.** You don't defend numbers from memory; you click the source, or you point at where the system already flagged the weakness.

### Three reflexes that win almost any hard question
1. **"Every number is one click from its source — let me show you."** Then click a citation chip; the PDF opens at the exact page, passage highlighted. This is the whole pitch in one gesture.
2. **"Our own fact-checker already flags that."** Faheem's risk note openly calls out its own beta shortcut, its unfinished WACC, even a net-cash discrepancy. When a judge finds a soft spot, you agree — and show that the system found it first. Honesty is the moat.
3. **"A human decides — Faheem never does."** Three visible gates; the IC agent is advisory-only. Whenever a question smells like "can we trust the AI?", route to the gates and the audit trail.

*Answers below are written to be spoken in 15–25 seconds. The **bold phrase** is the spine — if you blank, say just that.*

## Bucket 1 — TECHNICAL

### T1. "How do you stop the AI from making up numbers or citing documents that don't exist?"
**A:** We don't ask it politely — we enforce it. Every answer runs against the actual PDFs, and **the citation is generated by the model's own citation engine, not written by us: the quoted text has to physically exist on that page or it can't be cited.** So a fake reference is structurally impossible. Watch — I'll click one and the source opens right on the sentence.
**Point at:** Jahez chat → click a citation chip → PDF panel opens at the cited page, passage highlighted.
**If pressed:** And on top of that, our golden answers are human-reviewed and pinned, so the scripted path is doubly checked.

### T2. "No vector database, no RAG? Isn't retrieval the whole game in AI-for-finance?"
**A:** For a regulated use case, we made the opposite bet on purpose. **RAG guesses which chunks are relevant and can quietly drop the one that matters; we hand the model the whole curated, verified corpus and let it cite exact pages.** For a focused deal that's more accurate and fully auditable — no probabilistic middle layer to trust. And anything a user uploads joins the exact same engine.
**Point at:** the Sources accordion under an answer — every claim mapped to a document and page.
**If pressed:** RAG is the right tool when you have ten million documents; for one deal's document set, native citations beat retrieval on both accuracy and trust.

### T3. "Your wifi dies on stage — or the API is down. What happens?"
**A:** Nothing breaks. **The whole demo can run completely offline — we replay verified, recorded answers with the same streaming, same citations, same clickable sources.** There's a switch that shows you whether an answer came live or from our verified layer. Honestly, this isn't a fallback we're embarrassed about — a bank *wants* a deterministic, audited path.
**Point at:** the ⌘. overlay showing live/cached mode + cache status.
**If pressed:** Live follow-ups still work when the network's up — you're welcome to ask Faheem anything; it just takes 30–60 seconds while the agents read the filings.

### T4. "Which model is this, and what if a better one ships next month?"
**A:** We run a frontier model through the API today, but **the model is one line in our config — we can swap it without touching the product.** We're not betting the company on any single model; we're betting on the workflow and the governance around it. We also cache the whole document corpus, so repeat questions are fast and cheap.
**Point at:** (verbal) — the architecture slide, model-as-env-var.
**If pressed:** We even use a small, cheap model for the little "improve my prompt" helper — right model for each job.

### T5. "These are scanned PDFs in the real world — does your citation trick still work?"
**A:** Straight answer: **today we rely on the document's text layer, so a clean digital PDF is perfect and a pure image scan isn't — OCR is on our roadmap, not in the demo.** I'd rather tell you exactly where the line is than pretend. Most issuer filings and data-room docs are already digital text, which is why every number you'll see today resolves to a real page.
**Point at:** (verbal) — the Connections page roadmap items.
**If pressed:** OCR just feeds the same citation engine — it's an ingestion add-on, not a rebuild.

### T6. "A client's confidential documents go into your system. Where do they go, and do they train the model?"
**A:** **Client documents never train any model — full stop — and each company lives in its own isolated workspace with a full audit trail of every question, source, and file.** The uploaded doc is used to answer that firm's questions and nothing else. I'll be precise on labels: we're built to enterprise-grade practices, and formal certifications are honestly on our roadmap, not a badge I'll claim today.
**Point at:** the Audit Trail (grows during the demo) + per-workspace isolation.
**If pressed:** SSO and formal certifications are scoped to the roadmap — we're careful never to overclaim compliance to a room of bankers.

### T7. "Isn't this just a wrapper around some prompts?"
**A:** If it were, the numbers wouldn't be clickable. **Under the hood there's context routing that picks the right agent and document set, a citation transform that turns model output into deep-links, cache discipline so it's fast, and a generation engine that writes real Excel formulas — all under 463 automated tests.** The prompt is maybe five percent of it; the trust machinery is the product.
**Point at:** click through to the Excel model — live WACC and DCF formulas, every cell source-commented.
**If pressed:** "Prompt-only" can't open a PowerPoint deck in the client's brand with every figure traced to a page. That's engineering.

### T8. "Are those Live Model numbers recomputed live, or pre-baked for the demo?"
**A:** Live. **It's a pure TypeScript engine running in your browser — the exact same engine that builds the Excel model — and the base case is snapshot-gated so it can never silently drift.** Change an assumption and every dependent number (WACC, DCF, IRR, the sensitivity grid) recomputes in front of you, no server round-trip, no sleight of hand.
**Point at:** Live Model → a suggested chip → watch the numbers count up while the specialist team choreographs the recompute (Valuation → Critical Review → Compliance → Writing).
**If pressed:** The cached chat replay you saw earlier is scripted conversation; this is a different thing entirely — a real, deterministic calculation happening on this laptop, right now.

### T9. "Can I just tell it to change revenue?"
**A:** Try it — **sourced actuals are locked; only analyst assumptions are editable, and the system tells you exactly why when you push on the wrong line.** Every number in the model is one of two things: a sourced actual traced to a filing page, or a computed value with a formula and an input chain — only the second kind can move.
**Point at:** the command bar → "Change FY25 revenue to SAR 2 billion" → Critical Review refuses gracefully: "that figure is a sourced actual — source-locked."
**If pressed:** That's the two-tier provenance rule, not a UI restriction bolted on after the fact — the same whitelist gate a live judge what-if has to pass through too.

### T10. "Is that Sentiment card showing real social data?"
**A:** No, and we say so on the card itself: **it's a clearly labeled illustrative pack — synthetic, never presented as real scraped posts — and it never feeds the valuation or claims a sourced number.** It's a qualitative signal only: a label plus a one-line rationale, explicitly marked "signal only — not a valuation input."
**Point at:** the Sentiment card → "View the social pack" → every post tagged "illustrative demo data."
**If pressed:** Real social and alt-data feeds are a named connector on our roadmap page — we'd rather ship an honest placeholder than dress synthetic data up as real.

### T11. "What model wrote the draft IC email?"
**A:** No model wrote it. **It's a deterministic template that populates itself from the live model's current headline numbers, and a human reviews and sends it.** There's no generation step to hallucinate in — the numbers are pulled straight from the same engine you just watched recompute.
**Point at:** Draft to IC → the compose modal → edit any line → "Open in Outlook" — the human's own email client sends it.
**If pressed:** We could route the prose through the model later for polish, but for a number-bearing email to a committee, a template you can trust beats a generation you have to double-check.

## Bucket 2 — FINANCE

*You are not the quant in the room. Your job is: state it plainly, name the source, and where there's a shortcut, point out that the system flags it before they do.*

### F1. "Defend your WACC — where does 13.31% come from?"
**A:** **We build it bottom-up and every input traces to a dated source you can click.** The risk-free rate is the Saudi government sukuk yield, 4.60%; the equity risk premium is Damodaran's Saudi figure, 5.01%; times a sector beta of 1.82 — that gives roughly 13.31%. I won't pretend to be your quant, but I can show you the page behind each of those three numbers right now.
**Point at:** Excel → Assumptions tab → WACC build, each input carries a "Source: …, p." comment.
**If pressed:** And where an input isn't fully nailed down, the system says so — which is exactly your next question about beta.

### F2. "Your comps say the stock's worth ~24, your DCF says ~14. Which is it?"
**A:** **We show both on purpose — and we treat the comps as a sense-check, not the answer.** Our cash-flow model lands near SAR 14.4 against a market price of about 12.8. The peer multiples imply ~24, but those peers — US and European delivery names — simply trade richer, so we flag that gap rather than hide behind the flattering number. The honest read is the low end.
**Point at:** Excel → Comps tab (football-field range) next to the DCF output.
**If pressed:** Using the friendlier comps number to justify a Buy is exactly the kind of thing our fact-checker is built to catch.

### F3. "A beta of 1.82 — where did that come from, and why not the company's own?"
**A:** Great question, and here's the honest part: **Jahez's own traded beta is basically 0.02 — a thin-trading artifact, useless — so we used the average of two close peers, DoorDash and Delivery Hero, at 1.82. And our system openly flags on screen that a proper bottom-up beta is the next step, not something we've finished.** We took a defensible shortcut and labeled it as one.
**Point at:** the @Risk answer — read the line where Faheem flags the beta as an unresolved modeling step.
**If pressed:** That self-flag is the point: the tool tells the analyst where the work isn't done, instead of dressing an estimate up as fact.

### F4. "Your committee ranks Thara Pay above Jahez — but Jahez has the higher expected return. Explain."
**A:** Right, and that's the interesting call. **Jahez edges it on the blended return, 16.8 versus 16.2 — but Thara wins where it matters: it clears the hurdle by a wider margin, scores lower on risk, and its numbers rest on solid inputs, while Jahez's return leans on that unfinished WACC.** So it's reliability and cushion over a razor-thin return edge. And notice — Faheem argues that out loud, then hands the vote to the committee.
**Point at:** IC room → the comparison table + the ranking answer's rationale; the "Advisory only" disclaimer.
**If pressed:** Jahez also just printed a quarterly loss — a marginally higher model output on shakier ground isn't worth more than a cushioned, well-supported one.

### F5. "You call 55 million of losses 'one-off.' Is that real, or is that an excuse?"
**A:** Fair challenge — and it's itemized, each piece cited to the earnings call: **12.2 million on the Red Color portfolio, 11.8 million on Marn goodwill, 23.4 million of credit-loss provisions, and 8 million of Snoonu deal costs — 55.4 million.** And here's the part that wins the room: **ask Faheem this exact question.** Our own analyst pushes back — it treats only about 20 million as cleanly non-recurring and flags the credit losses as already repeating in Q1. We don't hide behind management's framing; the system audits it.
**Point at:** ⌘K → "Are the one-offs really one-off?" — let Faheem's recorded answer make the argument, then the earnings-call source (p.6) behind the decomposition.
**If pressed:** The one-offs explain the headline; the take-rate pressure is the real story — and it's why we're cautious, not bullish.

### F6. "What would actually flip your recommendation?"
**A:** **Two specific triggers, and we show them, not just say them.** One: nudge the WACC up about a point and Jahez's return drops toward the 15% hurdle — that's the whole margin gone. Two: if the price war compresses take-rates harder in the bear case, the story weakens further. We put both on a sensitivity grid so the committee sees the edges of the case, not just the base number.
**Point at:** Excel → Sensitivity tab (WACC × growth grid; take-rate × GMV grid).
**If pressed:** The base case sits only about 210 basis points above the hurdle — thin enough that we treat it as flagged, not cleared.

## Bucket 4 — GOVERNANCE

### G1. "Would a regulator — or a PIF — actually trust an AI in this seat?"
**A:** They can't adopt AI that decides — but they can adopt AI that's auditable, and that's the whole design. **Faheem never decides: there are three human gates, the committee agent is explicitly advisory-only, and there's a full audit trail of every answer, source, and document you've watched grow this whole demo.** The process stays theirs; we just make it a hundred times faster. That's what makes it adoptable at institutional scale.
**Point at:** the Audit Trail + the three human-gate markers across the pipeline.
**If pressed:** Auditable-and-fast is the only version of AI a pension fund or the PIF can actually put into a regulated process.

### G2. "What if the AI is simply wrong — live, in front of us?"
**A:** Then you catch it in one click, and that's exactly the safety property. **We don't claim Faheem is never wrong — we claim every single thing it says is checkable against its source, instantly.** An unverifiable AI is the danger; a fully citable one is safe *because* a human can falsify any claim on the spot. Try it — pick any number and click through.
**Point at:** any citation chip → source PDF at the cited page.
**If pressed:** Our system even flags its own weak spots — the beta, the WACC — before a reviewer does. Checkability is the feature, not damage control.

### G3. "If the committee acts on Faheem's analysis and it goes wrong — who's liable?"
**A:** **The decision — and its liability — stays exactly where it is today: with the investment committee. Faheem documents and accelerates; humans decide.** We're deliberately not in the decision seat. The tool produces a fully sourced, auditable file; the committee votes, on the record. We move the work from weeks to hours; we don't move the accountability.
**Point at:** IC room → persistent "Advisory only — the investment decision rests with the committee."
**If pressed:** Any override of a mandate limit needs an explicit, minuted committee vote — the governance is in the workflow, not just the disclaimer.

## Never say (on stage, ever)

- **A competitor's name.** Not Rogo, Hebbia, or AlphaSense. Say *"the category leaders emerging in the US and UK."*
- **A certification you don't hold.** No "SOC 2 certified," "ISO," "CMA-licensed," "Shariah-certified." Say *"built to enterprise-grade practices; formal certifications are on our roadmap."*
- **"The AI decides"** / "Faheem recommends you invest." Always *"advisory — the committee decides."*
- **"It never hallucinates"** / "our data can't be wrong." Say *"every claim is checkable to its source — that's the safety property."*
- **A claimed customer or signed deal.** Alinma Capital is a *design-partner framing*; the PIF is *aspiration, not a customer.*
- **"Our integrations are live."** SAHMK / Tadawul / GASTAT / open banking are *connectors on the roadmap*; today it's *public disclosures + uploads.*
- **"We handle scanned documents."** Say *"text-layer today; OCR is on the roadmap."*
- **A finance number you're unsure of.** Don't wing it — say *"let me pull the source,"* and click the citation. The click is always a stronger answer than a guess.
